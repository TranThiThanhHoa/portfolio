VR Driving Simulator — Autonomous Vehicles

Overview

This project focuses on evaluating driver interaction with autonomous vehicle systems in a virtual reality environment. The simulator provides an immersive platform to study human factors in autonomous driving scenarios.

Problem

Autonomous vehicles present unique challenges in human-machine interaction. Drivers need to understand and trust AV systems, especially during transitions between automated and manual control. Current research lacks comprehensive studies on trust-building mechanisms in VR environments.

Research Question

How do changes in Heads-Up Display (HUD) information affect driver trust and situational awareness in autonomous vehicle scenarios within a VR driving simulator?

Concept

The VR driving simulator integrates Unity-based 3D environments with eye-tracking technology to monitor driver attention and trust indicators. The system dynamically adjusts HUD information to simulate various AV communication strategies.

Experiment

Participants experienced different HUD information conditions while operating the VR simulator. Eye-tracking data from Tobii devices captured gaze patterns, and subjective trust measures were collected through post-experiment surveys.

Results

The study revealed significant correlations between HUD information clarity and driver trust levels. Eye-tracking data showed improved situational awareness with optimized information presentation, leading to more confident driver-AV interactions.

Impact

Findings contribute to the development of better human-machine interfaces for autonomous vehicles. The research informs design guidelines for HUD systems and VR-based training platforms for future mobility solutions.
